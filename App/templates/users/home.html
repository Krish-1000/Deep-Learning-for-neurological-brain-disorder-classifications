Brain disorder


classification

Brain disorders encompass a range of conditions that affect the brain's structure and function. These disorders can result in various physical, cognitive, and emotional symptoms.

Mild stage

A mild brain disorder involves subtle changes in brain function, often affecting memory, mood, or cognitive abilities. Symptoms may include slight forgetfulness, minor confusion, or mild difficulties in concentrating. Early intervention can help manage these symptoms and slow progression.


Moderate_Impairment Stage

Moderate impairment in brain disorders involves noticeable difficulties with cognitive functions such as memory, reasoning, and problem-solving. Individuals may struggle with daily tasks, require assistance, and experience significant changes in behavior or personality. This stage often necessitates increased support to maintain quality of life.

No_Impairment

The "No Impairment" stage of brain disorders, such as Alzheimer's, is characterized by the absence of noticeable symptoms. Individuals in this stage do not exhibit any memory loss or cognitive decline, and they perform daily activities without difficulty. This stage is often undetectable without advanced medical testing.

Very_Mild_Impairment Stage

Very Mild Impairment Stage in brain disorders is characterized by subtle but noticeable difficulties in cognitive functions, such as memory lapses and slight challenges in problem-solving. Individuals can still perform daily activities independently but may struggle with complex tasks. This stage often requires close monitoring to track progression and implement early interventions.

WHAT IS A CNN ?

Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) are a class of deep learning algorithms primarily used for image and video recognition, classification, and analysis. They work by automatically and adaptively learning spatial hierarchies of features from input images through a series of layers.

WHAT IS A ANN?

Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs) are a class of machine learning models inspired by the structure and function of the human brain. They consist of interconnected layers of nodes (neurons) that process and learn from data.
